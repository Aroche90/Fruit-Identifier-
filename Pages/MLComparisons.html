Machine Learning Models

Here is a summary of each of our machine learning modules, including their performance. To learn more about the process taken for each of these, please click on the links below, or navigate to each via the drop down bar above. Happy (Machine) Learning!

Sequential Model

  • Accuracy: 10.8-11%
  • Advantages:
    • Plenty of options for your model--can study to optimize performance
    • Fast learning model that won't kill your processor
  • Disadvantages:
    • Images with white space can (and will) effect your model
  • Recommendation: Make sure to do your research first!

Logistic Regression

  • Accuracy: 100% (overfit)
  • Advantages:
    • Ease of use.
    • Reports are built in.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Model is overfit
  • Recommendation: Good to go.

Recurrent Neural Networks:

  • Accuracy: 92%
  • Advantages:
    • Uses previous inputs to determine output.
    • Good at predicting what is going to happen next, but not necessarily reading a static image.
  • Disadvantages:
    • CPU Intensive
  • Recommendation: Good for this easily predictable file structure of images.

k-Nearest Neighbours

  • Accuracy: less than 1%
  • Advantages:
    • None. It feels like a false illusion; one would think that when images cluster, grapes would identify with other grapes, but that's not the case when it is broken down by pixels and saturation.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Pixel Histograms: Lots of color overlap between fruits.
    • Identified only by color, saturation, & hue, not by shape.
    • Needs pictures to be labeled properly.
  • Recommendation: Not for image identification.
